About Mumbai

Mumbai, the capital of Maharashtra, is India’s financial, entertainment, and cultural hub. Known as the "City of Dreams," it is home to Bollywood, historic landmarks, street food, and vibrant nightlife, making it a must-visit destination for travelers.

Mumbai, the financial capital of India, is a vibrant city known for its historical landmarks, bustling markets, Bollywood glamour, stunning beaches, and delicious street food. It is a city of contrasts, where colonial-era architecture stands beside modern skyscrapers, and where every corner tells a story of dreams and ambition.

Why Visit Mumbai?

The Gateway of India - is one of Mumbai’s most famous landmarks, located on the waterfront in Colaba. Built in 1924, it is a historic monument of colonial heritage and a popular tourist attraction.

The Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Vastu Sangrahalaya (CSMVS) - formerly known as the Prince of Wales Museum, is one of India’s finest museums, showcasing a vast collection of art, history, and culture. Located in South Mumbai, near the Gateway of India, this museum is a must-visit for history lovers, art enthusiasts, and cultural explorers.

The Elephanta Caves - located on Elephanta Island in Mumbai Harbour, are a collection of ancient rock-cut cave temples dedicated to Lord Shiva. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is one of the most fascinating historical and architectural wonders in India, attracting tourists, history lovers, and adventure seekers.
