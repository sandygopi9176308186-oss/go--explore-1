About Shimla

Shimla, the capital of Himachal Pradesh, is one of India's most popular hill stations. Surrounded by lush green forests, snow-capped peaks, and colonial-era buildings, it offers a perfect mix of history, culture, and adventure. It was once the summer capital of British India, which is why you can still see beautiful colonial architecture throughout the city.

Shimla, the capital of Himachal Pradesh, is a beautiful hill station in northern India. Known for its colonial architecture, lush green hills, and pleasant climate, it is a popular tourist destination.

Why Visit Shimla?

Jakhoo Temple - is a famous Hindu temple in Shimla, Himachal Pradesh, dedicated to Lord Hanuman. It is situated at Jakhoo Hill, the highest point in Shimla, at an altitude of 2,455 meters (8,054 feet) above sea level.

Christ Church - is one of the most iconic landmarks in Shimla, Himachal Pradesh. Built in 1857, it is the second-oldest church in North India and a stunning example of neo-Gothic architecture.

Chadwick Falls - is a mesmerizing waterfall located near Shimla, Himachal Pradesh, surrounded by dense forests and lush greenery. It is a popular natural attraction, offering a peaceful retreat for nature lovers and adventure seekers.
